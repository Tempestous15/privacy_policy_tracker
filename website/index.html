What a site claims, and what it actually does — before you agree to anything

ClipPri is a browser extension that reads a site's privacy policy and checks what it's actually doing on your device — instantly, entirely on your device. No account, no server, nothing you read or detect ever leaves your browser.

Disclosed

What the policy says

Red flags, ToS;DR ratings, and an on-device summary — all readings of the privacy policy the site published.

vs.

Observed

What we can technically detect

Third-party trackers actually contacted on the site, checked against DuckDuckGo's Tracker Radar dataset — independent of anything the policy claims.

We show these side by side, never blended into one score. A browser extension can't see a company's internal data handling — only what it discloses and what's technically observable. When the two disagree, we flag that first.

🚩 Instant red flags

A local, on-device scan flags data selling, vague third-party sharing, indefinite retention, arbitration clauses, and more — the moment you open a page.

🌐 ToS;DR ratings

Where available, community-reviewed ratings from Terms of Service; Didn't Read are shown alongside the local scan for well-known sites.

🧠 On-device AI summary

A small language model runs locally in your browser (via WebGPU) to generate a plain-English summary — no policy text is ever sent to us or anyone else.

📡 Live tracker detection

Watches which third-party domains a site actually contacts as you browse, and checks them against a bundled Tracker Radar index — categorized in plain language, not just a score.
